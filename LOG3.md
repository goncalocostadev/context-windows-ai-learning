## Key Takeaways

* Explicit conversions are intentional; implicit conversions happen automatically.
* `===` compares both type and value, while `==` may perform type coercion.
* Truthy and falsy are not the same as `true` and `false`.
* Empty arrays (`[]`) and empty objects (`{}`) are truthy because they are objects.
* The modulo operator (`%`) returns the remainder after division and is commonly used to determine whether a number is even or odd.
* Logical operators combine boolean expressions:

  * `&&` requires both expressions to be true.
  * `||` requires at least one expression to be true.
  * `!` reverses a boolean value.
* Breaking complex expressions into smaller parts makes them easier to evaluate and debug.
